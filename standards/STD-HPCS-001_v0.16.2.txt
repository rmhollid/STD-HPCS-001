# 2. PURPOSE_SCOPE
This document defines a high-pressure, high-temperature, solid-state embedded material element that carries environmental state through Raman-active, ferroaxial, axial-domain, or chiral optical response.

The active scope is limited to:
- theory: how a rugged ordered material can carry pressure, temperature, strain, damage, and domain state as an optical response;
- construction: the material stack, protective matrix, active inclusion, infrared optical path, calibration structure, and readout channel;
- operation: how an embedded element is interrogated and how its infrared, Raman, polarization, or thermal optical response is converted into state information.

Infrared-laser operation is an explicit branch of this standard. It covers near-infrared and infrared excitation, fiber delivery, standoff delivery, window-coupled delivery, Raman peak-shift sensing, Stokes/anti-Stokes temperature sensing, polarization-channel readout, and absorption-style process-state readout.

Solid-state heat-extraction operation is an explicit branch of this standard. It covers passive solid-state heat extraction, phonon-polariton heat highways, thermal-infrared radiative dumping, thermophotonic-assisted heat pumping, and chiral or ferroaxial heat-path readout. Heat extraction claims shall identify the heat source, transport carrier, heat sink, gradient or work input, loss path, and saturation behavior.

Field-assisted heat-transport operation is an explicit branch of this standard. It covers radio-frequency telemetry and modulation, microwave magnetic or dielectric control, magnon-assisted thermal-state transport, near-field electromagnetic heat transfer, thermophotovoltaic receiver conversion, time-modulated photonic heat pumping, and fluctuation-mediated quantum thermal channels. Field-assisted claims shall identify the field band, coupled carrier, mode class, active or passive status, heat source, protected region, heat sink, work input when required, loss budget, saturation behavior, survivability limit, and measured protected-zone temperature reduction against the unmodified host baseline.

Internal-lasing operation is an optional explicit branch of this standard. It covers embedded Raman-laser conversion, random-laser composite emission, rare-earth ceramic microcavity emission, semiconductor or quantum-well gain layers, phonon-polariton coherent-emitter candidates, and thermal-threshold optical beacons. Internal lasing claims shall identify gain medium, pump path, feedback mechanism, threshold condition, output path, residual-heat dump, loss budget, and survivability limits.


Thermal-mode trap storage is an explicit candidate branch of this standard. It covers high-retention mode traps, virtual-cavity energy locks, phase-change or metastable material reservoirs, anti-thermalization barriers, controlled release triggers, and storage accounting. A trap-storage claim shall identify capture path, storage state, anti-thermalization mechanism, hold interval, leakage path, release trigger, useful output channel, and damage limit.

Truth classification is an explicit branch-governance layer of this standard. Each physics branch, construction claim, operating mode, material candidate, and deployment claim shall carry a truth class, material-readiness tag, and operating-regime tag. These labels separate supported physics from engineering rules, candidate device branches, theory-only branches, material-qualification requirements, proof gates, disproof gates, and indeterminate-status gates.

This document does not define an actuator, propulsion component, biological sensor, chemical detector, or electronic transistor. It may define an embedded heat-extraction, heat-transport, or internal optical-emission element only when the transport carrier, sink, gain subsystem, and operating envelope are explicit. The primary device class remains an embedded optical witness and thermal-state material.

# 3. DEFINITIONS
Active inclusion: the material grain, film, crystal island, ceramic phase, or fiber-tip coating that produces the measurable optical response.

Axial-domain order: a signed structural domain state represented by an axial-vector orientation or equivalent signed rotational state.

Chiral optical response: difference in optical response between opposite handed or circular polarization channels.

Embedded optical witness: a solid-state material element placed inside or on a host component so that later optical interrogation reveals the local environmental state or exposure history.

Ferroaxial material: material with a structural axial-vector order parameter. Ferroaxial order may support signed optical response but shall not be treated as structural chirality by itself.

Harsh environment: operating condition involving elevated temperature, elevated pressure, high strain, cyclic stress, corrosive adjacency, radiation adjacency, or restricted access after installation.

Host component: the structural body that contains or supports the active inclusion.

Optical readout path: laser, fiber, window, waveguide, or free-space path used to interrogate the active inclusion.

Pressure coefficient: change in mode frequency per unit pressure for a calibrated Raman or optical mode.

Raman marker mode: spectrally resolvable vibrational mode used as a readout channel.

Raman optical activity channel: polarization-sensitive Raman channel that can report signed domain response or helicity contrast.

State vector X_env: bounded environmental and material state inferred from the measured optical response.

Thermal survival limit T_surv: highest temperature at which the active inclusion remains chemically, structurally, and optically usable after exposure.

Operating temperature T_op: temperature at the active inclusion during interrogation or during exposure.

Operating pressure P_op: pressure or equivalent compressive stress at the active inclusion.

Calibration curve: deterministic mapping between measured optical features and environmental state variables within a qualified operating envelope.

Infrared excitation lane: selected excitation wavelength family used for readout, including 785 nm, 830 nm, 980 nm, 1064 nm, 1310 nm, 1550 nm, or mid-infrared bands when qualified.

IR laser readout: optical interrogation mode in which a near-infrared or infrared laser is used to excite a Raman, absorption, reflection, polarization, or thermal optical response from the embedded active inclusion.

Raman efficiency tradeoff: wavelength-selection rule that shorter excitation wavelengths generally improve Raman scattering strength while longer wavelengths generally reduce fluorescence background and may improve fiber/process compatibility.

Stokes/anti-Stokes temperature ratio: Raman temperature-readout method that estimates temperature from the relative intensity of thermally populated anti-Stokes scattering and Stokes scattering.

Fiber delivery path: optical route that delivers excitation light and collects return signal through silica, specialty, or sapphire-compatible fiber or fiber-adjacent optics.

Sapphire optical path: high-temperature optical window, fiber, or substrate lane based on sapphire or related wide-energy-band optical material.

Witness mode: readout mode that may record exposure history even when the environment is later removed.

Heat extraction path: a designed solid-state route by which thermal energy moves from a hot region toward a colder sink, radiator, converter, or actively driven pump stage.

Thermal pickup layer: material region thermally coupled to the hot zone and selected to couple lattice heat, infrared emission, or phonon energy into the transport carrier.

Heat sink: colder body, radiator, coolant interface, thermophotovoltaic converter, sacrificial thermal mass, or driven pump dump region that accepts extracted heat.

Phonon-polariton: coupled infrared electromagnetic and lattice-vibration mode supported by a polar material.

Heat transport carrier: named carrier responsible for thermal-energy movement, including phonon conduction, coherent phonon packets, surface phonon-polaritons, hyperbolic phonon-polaritons, infrared waveguide modes, or radiative photons.

Thermal-infrared radiative dump: branch in which heat is converted to infrared radiation and directed toward a sink, radiator, converter, or optical escape path.

Thermophotonic assisted heat pump: branch in which external work or a driven photonic/electronic stage assists heat transfer against, or beyond, passive thermal flow.

Passive solid-state heat extraction: heat movement from a hotter embedded region to a colder region through solid conduction, phonon-polariton transport, or radiative transfer without a work-driven pump.

Spectral heat-extraction mode: operating mode selected by electromagnetic band, coupling mechanism, transport carrier, loss model, and sink interface.

Heat-path readout: optical, Raman, polarization, thermal, or reference-channel measurement used to verify that the heat-extraction path is intact and operating within envelope.

Radio-frequency control field: low-frequency or radio-frequency electromagnetic field used for telemetry, modulation, inductive coupling, or state interrogation rather than primary heat extraction.

Microwave control field: electromagnetic field in the microwave band used for dielectric calibration, magnetic resonance control, magnon excitation, or thermal-valve operation.

Magnon: collective spin-wave excitation that can carry spin and thermal information in qualified magnetic materials.

Spin-caloritronic layer: material layer that couples thermal gradients, spin currents, magnetic order, or electrical readout.

Near-field electromagnetic heat transfer: heat transfer mediated by evanescent electromagnetic modes across a sub-wavelength spacing or closely coupled interface.

Evanescent thermal channel: non-propagating electromagnetic field component that can transfer thermal energy between nearby bodies when geometry and material response support coupling.

Fluctuation-mediated quantum thermal channel: thermal transport channel described by fluctuational electrodynamics, in which thermal and quantum electromagnetic fluctuations couple heat between material bodies.

Thermophotovoltaic receiver: converter layer that receives thermal photons and converts part of the received energy into electrical output while dumping residual heat.

Time-modulated photonic heat pump: active heat-control branch in which an externally driven time-varying optical or electromagnetic property changes radiative heat flow.

Casimir-adjacent spacing constraint: mechanical spacing constraint relevant to near-field spacings where surface forces, vibration, contamination, or contact collapse can alter thermal coupling.

Field-assisted heat transport: use of radio, microwave, electromagnetic, magnonic, photonic, or fluctuation-mediated channels to control, read, enhance, suppress, or pump heat flow.

Protected thermal region: host region whose temperature, heat flux, or survivability margin is the target of heat removal.

Unmodified host baseline: measured or bounded heat-flow and protected-region temperature behavior of the same host geometry without the HPCS transport, extraction, or field-assisted layer.

Protected-zone cooling metric: difference between protected-region temperature or heat flux in the unmodified baseline and the same quantity with the HPCS heat path active.

Exported heat q_exported: heat delivered to a sink, radiator, converter, coolant interface, or pump dump outside the protected thermal region.

Internal parasitic heat q_parasitic_internal: heat added inside the protected thermal region by field absorption, dielectric loss, magnetic loss, optical absorption, contact loss, or local control hardware.

External parasitic heat q_parasitic_external: heat generated outside the protected thermal region by drive electronics, sources, detectors, conversion losses, or pump support hardware.

Truth class: normative label assigning a statement or branch to one of SUPPORTED_PHYSICS, ENGINEERING_STANDARD_RULE, CANDIDATE_DEVICE_BRANCH, THEORY_ONLY_BRANCH, REQUIRES_MATERIAL_QUALIFICATION, UNPROVEN, PROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE, DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE, INDETERMINATE, or NEEDS_DISCRIMINATING_TEST.

SUPPORTED_PHYSICS: branch or mechanism with established scientific basis outside this project, while still requiring project-specific geometry and material qualification before deployment.

ENGINEERING_STANDARD_RULE: rule created by this document to prevent overclaim, enforce accounting, define interfaces, or qualify operation.

CANDIDATE_DEVICE_BRANCH: physically plausible construction branch not yet qualified as a deployment device in this project.

THEORY_ONLY_BRANCH: branch carried only as a theoretical or modeling path until experimental or construction proof is supplied.

REQUIRES_MATERIAL_QUALIFICATION: branch or material family that cannot be promoted by name and must pass the material screening, operating-regime, and validation gates in this document.

UNPROVEN: label for claims that shall remain preserved as candidates until the required proof vector is supplied.

PROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE: label for claims demonstrated inside a declared material stack, geometry, operating regime, measurement method, and uncertainty bound.

DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE: label for claims tested and not supported inside a declared material stack, geometry, operating regime, measurement method, and uncertainty bound. This status shall not disprove other envelopes.

INDETERMINATE: label for claims where evidence is incomplete, ambiguous, conflicting, or outside measurement certainty.

NEEDS_DISCRIMINATING_TEST: label for claims where two or more hypotheses remain plausible and a next test is needed to distinguish them.

CTI_HPCS proof surface: deterministic comparison surface used to score competing HPCS hypotheses inside a declared material stack, geometry, operating regime, measurement method, and uncertainty bound.

CTI_HPCS_PROOF_RECORD: record that stores candidate hypotheses, evidence inputs, scoring surface, hypothesis scores, winning hypothesis, claim status, uncertainty bound, scope of proof or disproof, and next discriminating test.

Disproven scope: bounded envelope in which a claim has been tested and not supported. Disproof shall not be generalized outside the declared material, geometry, operating regime, measurement method, and uncertainty bound.

Claim advancement rule: rule that determines whether a claim remains UNPROVEN, becomes PROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE, becomes DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE, remains INDETERMINATE, or requires NEEDS_DISCRIMINATING_TEST.

Material-readiness tag: label assigning a material or construction to PROVEN_BASIS, CANDIDATE, or DEPLOYMENT_QUALIFIED.

PROVEN_BASIS: material or mechanism has an established external scientific basis, but this tag does not by itself qualify harsh embedded deployment.

CANDIDATE: material or construction may be screened for this standard but has not passed all deployment gates.

DEPLOYMENT_QUALIFIED: material or construction has passed the declared survivability, compatibility, transport, calibration, cycling, and safety gates for the declared use envelope.

Operating-regime tag: label assigning the declared operating envelope to ROOM_TEMP, CRYOGENIC, HIGH_TEMP_CANDIDATE, PRESSURE_CANDIDATE, or DEPLOYMENT_QUALIFIED.

Field mode class: classification of a field-assisted mode as SUPPORT_CONTROL, PASSIVE_TRANSPORT, CONVERSION, ACTIVE_CONTROL, ACTIVE_PUMP_CANDIDATE, or ACTIVE_PUMP.

Residual heat dump: declared path by which unconverted heat from a thermophotovoltaic receiver, converter, or pump stage is marked DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE away from the protected thermal region.

Magnonic stability margin: temperature, pressure, and field-distance margin between the operating point and collapse or unacceptable drift of the magnetic phase needed for magnon-assisted operation.

Modulation law: declared time dependence of a driven photonic, electromagnetic, thermal, or material property used by an active heat-control or active-pump branch.

Control volume CV_protected: explicitly bounded thermal region around the protected hot zone used for heat-balance accounting. It shall define boundary surface A_boundary, volume V_protected, measurement points, and averaging interval.

Control-volume boundary heat q_boundary: heat crossing the boundary of CV_protected, signed positive when leaving the protected region.

Transient storage term dU_dt: time rate of internal-energy storage inside CV_protected. During transient operation, apparent cooling shall not be accepted unless dU_dt is measured, bounded, or driven toward steady-state acceptance.

Steady-state acceptance window: interval during which protected-zone temperature, source load, sink state, field input, and stored-energy term remain within declared bounds.

Baseline equivalence rule: requirement that the unmodified host baseline and active HPCS trial use equivalent geometry, boundary conditions, heat load, sink condition, ambient condition, pressure state, strain state, measurement locations, and time window.

COP_protected: coefficient of performance based on heat removed from CV_protected after internal parasitic heating is deducted.

COP_device: coefficient of performance based on exported heat at the external sink or dump after internal and external parasitic heating are counted.

Readout parasitic heat: heat deposited by measurement light, RF, microwave, detector-adjacent hardware, pump leakage, or reference-channel operation. It shall be assigned as q_readout_internal or q_readout_external according to where it is deposited.

Near-field spacing margin M_nf: formal margin between qualified spacing d_spacing and the declared upper near-field spacing limit d_nf_max.

Material heat-extraction screening vector: declared material screen including survivability, thermal conductivity, infrared phonon band, emissivity, polariton support, pressure stability, chemical compatibility, and readout compatibility.

Heat term unit convention: every q_* heat-flow term is expressed in watts W unless explicitly typed otherwise; stored energy U and q_stored are expressed in joules J; dU_dt is expressed in watts W; COP_protected, COP_device, eta_heat_passive, heat_burden_coverage, and all eta_* ratios are dimensionless.

Storage release debit q_storage_release: positive heat-flow debit equal to max(-dU_dt,0) used when stored energy inside CV_protected is being released and could otherwise be mistaken for sustained heat extraction.

Sustained protected net heat removal q_net_protected_sustained: time-averaged protected-zone net heat removal accepted only after storage accumulation, storage release, internal parasitic heat, and readout heat are debited across the steady-state acceptance window.

Heat burden coverage: dimensionless ratio heat_burden_coverage = q_net_protected_sustained / q_source used to state what fraction of the protected heat source is actually handled by the HPCS path.

Passive heat-transfer efficiency eta_heat_passive: dimensionless passive export ratio q_exported / q_source for gradient-driven heat extraction only. It shall not be used as a substitute for COP in active heat-pump claims.

Worst-case near-field spacing d_spacing_worst: maximum effective spacing across tolerance, roughness, vibration, thermal expansion, pressure deformation, contamination, and service drift.

Worst-case near-field margin M_nf_worst: margin M_nf_worst = d_nf_max - d_spacing_worst. Noncontact near-field operation requires M_nf_worst > 0.

Modulation stability bound: bounded range for a time-modulated property, including modulation amplitude A_mod, modulation frequency f_mod, duty cycle D_mod, drive heat deposition q_drive_heat, and proof-status condition for runaway heating or self-heating.

Exported thermal state vector: interface record containing protected-zone temperature, heat flux, heat burden coverage, saturation state, storage state, and survivability margin for consumers such as STD-SPSA, STD-DRIVE, STD-HULL, or STD-SHIP.

Exported heat-path state vector: interface record containing carrier identity, path integrity, attenuation, sink state, baseline equivalence status, and proof-status state.

Exported optical record vector: interface record containing wavelength band, Raman/IR/polarization features, readout power, readout parasitic heat, signal quality, and calibration identifier.

Exported material stack vector: interface record containing active inclusion, transport layer, barrier layer, host coupling layer, sink/dump interface, and survivability screen.

Exported proof-status state vector: interface record containing the tested criterion, measured decisive term, proof/disproof cause, uncertainty bound, and safe fallback classification.

Internal laser: coherent or laser-like emission generated inside the HPCS medium or an embedded HPCS-compatible gain inclusion.

Gain medium: material subsystem that provides optical, Raman, rare-earth, semiconductor, polariton, or scattering-assisted gain.

Pump path: declared input path supplying energy to the gain medium through optical, electrical, thermal-assisted, Raman, near-field, RF, microwave, or field-assisted coupling.

Feedback mechanism: cavity, microcavity, distributed feedback, waveguide resonance, scattering feedback, polariton resonance, fiber loop, protected external cavity, or equivalent feedback path that permits stimulated emission above threshold.

Laser threshold: minimum pump condition at which net gain exceeds total losses or equivalent random/polariton gain exceeds emission threshold.

Residual laser heat: heat remaining in the host, gain medium, pump path, receiver, or dump after useful optical output is produced.

Raman laser mode: laser emission generated by stimulated Raman scattering in a Raman-active solid, ceramic, crystal, fiber, or waveguide.

Random laser mode: laser-like emission in a disordered gain medium where multiple scattering supplies feedback rather than a conventional mirror cavity.

Phonon-polariton coherent-emitter candidate: coherent-output candidate based on coupled photon-optical-phonon modes in a polar material or heterostructure.

Thermal-threshold optical beacon: emission subsystem that activates when heat, pressure, strain, or damage crosses a declared threshold.


High-retention mode trap: [CANDIDATE_DEVICE_BRANCH / REQUIRES_MATERIAL_QUALIFICATION] declared material configuration that may delay energy decay by confining energy in selected modes or material states only when measured retention exceeds the declared application threshold. It shall not be described as perfect or lossless.

Thermal-mode trap storage: [CANDIDATE_DEVICE_BRANCH] candidate storage branch in which thermal energy may be captured into selected modes or material states, retained for a declared interval, and released through a controlled output channel only after capture, retention, release, and energy-balance tests pass.

Virtual cavity energy lock: [THEORY_ONLY_BRANCH until tested] effective resonator, interference state, domain boundary, scattering loop, polariton confinement region, or photonic/polaritonic mode condition proposed to select or gate energy without requiring a literal mirror cavity. It shall be treated as selector, buffer, feedback path, or release coupler unless a storage proof vector demonstrates battery-scale retention.

Storage state type: [ENGINEERING_STANDARD_RULE] declared physical reservoir proposed to hold stored energy. Allowed candidates include latent heat, metastable phase, trapped charge, defect excitation, strain state, domain state, spin state, chemical potential, or short-duration optical/polaritonic field energy, but each candidate requires its own proof vector.

Anti-thermalization barrier: [REQUIRES_MATERIAL_QUALIFICATION] declared material feature proposed to reduce uncontrolled decay of a selected storage state back into random lattice heat. It is accepted only when leakage-to-heat is measured or bounded below the declared threshold.

Capture mode: [REQUIRES_TEST] declared mechanism by which heat, phonons, photons, polaritons, excitations, or field energy may enter the storage state. It is accepted only when measured capture exceeds uncontrolled leakage during the charge interval.

Passive leakage path: uncontrolled energy loss from the storage state into heat, radiation, defects, host damage, or parasitic modes.

Controlled release trigger: declared thermal, optical, RF, microwave, pressure, strain, electrical, magnetic, phase-boundary, or cavity-detuning condition that opens a useful release channel.

Useful release channel: declared channel through which stored energy exits as heat to a sink, infrared radiation, narrowband light, coherent light, electrical output, Raman output, mechanical work indicator, or state readout.

Storage retention ratio R_hold: fraction of stored energy remaining after a declared hold interval.

Trap-storage safety limit: maximum allowed stored energy, local temperature, pressure, stress, phase fraction, or emission burst energy before the storage branch is marked unsafe or DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE for the declared envelope.

# 4. SYSTEM_OVERVIEW
The system is a rugged solid-state optical state element embedded into a host material or attached as a protected witness layer. The active inclusion carries environmental state by changing measurable optical features under pressure, temperature, strain, damage, or domain-state changes.

The central operating chain is:
1. the host component experiences temperature, pressure, strain, or damage;
2. the embedded active inclusion shares the local environment through mechanical, thermal, or chemical coupling;
3. Raman-active or polarization-sensitive modes shift, broaden, split, invert sign, or change intensity;
4. an optical path interrogates the active inclusion;
5. measured spectra are converted into a bounded state vector;
6. the state vector reports live condition, exposure history, or component identity.

The preferred project use is a passive or semi-passive embedded witness material for pressure, strain, temperature, overload, domain state, thermal history, and structural health.

The infrared-laser operating chain is:
1. an external infrared or near-infrared source generates the interrogation beam;
2. the beam is routed by fiber, window, waveguide, or standoff optics;
3. the active inclusion receives the beam without exceeding local heating or damage limits;
4. Raman, polarization, reflection, transmission, absorption, or thermal optical response is collected;
5. the response is filtered against fluorescence, thermal glow, fiber background, and window background;
6. calibrated features are converted into X_env.


The heat-extraction operating chain is:
1. a hot embedded region generates lattice heat, infrared radiation, or both;
2. the thermal pickup layer couples heat into a named carrier;
3. the carrier moves energy through a solid transport layer or radiative path;
4. the transport layer delivers energy to a colder shell, radiator, coolant interface, converter, or active pump dump;
5. optical or thermal readout verifies path health, saturation state, and local environmental condition.

The heat-extraction branch shall not claim heat removal unless the heat sink or work-driven pump stage is identified. Chiral, ferroaxial, or signed optical response may be used as a heat-path state readout or routing indicator, but shall not be treated as the primary heat carrier unless a specific carrier model is declared.

The field-assisted operating chain is:
1. a radio, microwave, infrared, near-field, magnetic, or time-modulated field interacts with a qualified material layer;
2. the field couples to a declared carrier such as magnon, phonon-polariton, evanescent photon channel, electronic carrier, or photonic receiver state;
3. the carrier modifies heat flow, enables heat-path readout, opens or closes a thermal valve, or assists heat pumping;
4. extracted or transported heat is delivered to a declared sink, radiator, converter, coolant interface, or pump dump;
5. readout confirms heat-path state, field-coupling state, and saturation behavior.

Field-assisted heat transport shall not be treated as vacuum energy extraction. Quantum-field language in this standard is limited to fluctuation-mediated thermal transport, evanescent electromagnetic coupling, time-modulated photonic work input, and measurable heat-flow control between material bodies.

# 5. ARCHITECTURE
## 5.1 DEVICE_ARCHITECTURE
The device architecture contains:
- host component;
- mechanical coupling region;
- thermal coupling region;
- chemical barrier or compatibility layer;
- active inclusion;
- optical access path;
- readout instrument;
- calibration model;
- state reconstruction algorithm.

The active inclusion shall remain solid-state during operation and shall not require moving parts.

## 5.2 MATERIAL_ARCHITECTURE
The active material shall be selected from one or more of the following families:
- ferroaxial oxide or ceramic;
- axial-domain crystalline material;
- wide-energy-band Raman-active ceramic;
- high-temperature-compatible Raman marker crystal;
- pressure-stable crystalline inclusion;
- thin-film material with domain-stable optical response.

Layered low-temperature charge-density-wave materials may be used as theory templates, but harsh embedded applications shall prioritize materials with high thermal stability, pressure stability, chemical compatibility, and repeatable optical calibration.

Composition expansion rule:
A material family shall not be promoted by family name alone. Each candidate shall declare composition, phase form, intended carrier, operating role, truth class, material-readiness tag, operating-regime tag, and proof/disproof trigger.

## 5.6 INFRARED_WAVELENGTH_LANES
Permitted wavelength lanes are:
- 785 nm: high Raman efficiency, higher fluorescence risk;
- 830 nm: Raman-compatible compromise lane;
- 980 nm: fiber-compatible near-IR lane with moderate Raman efficiency;
- 1064 nm: reduced fluorescence lane with lower Raman scattering than shorter wavelengths;
- 1310 nm: telecom-compatible low-loss fiber lane for selected optical readout;
- 1550 nm: telecom-compatible lane for fiber-network concepts and selected absorption or backscatter modes;
- mid-IR: absorption/process-state lane where Raman is not the primary mechanism.

The selected lane shall be justified against fluorescence, thermal background, optical attenuation, detector sensitivity, Raman efficiency, fiber/window survivability, and host-material absorption.

## 5.7 IR_EMBEDDED_FORMS
Infrared-compatible embedded forms include:
- fiber-tip witness coating;

## 5.8 HEAT_EXTRACTION_ARCHITECTURE
The heat-extraction architecture contains:
- hot-zone pickup interface;
- thermal pickup layer;
- transport layer;
- optional polariton or infrared coupling layer;
- optional chiral, ferroaxial, or signed-domain readout layer;
- cold-side heat sink, radiator, coolant interface, converter, or pump dump;
- heat-path readout channel;
- saturation and failure monitor.

Preferred passive stack:
HOT_ZONE -> THERMAL_PICKUP_LAYER -> PHONON_OR_POLARITON_TRANSPORT_LAYER -> COLD_SIDE_DUMP.

Preferred assisted stack:
HOT_ZONE -> THERMAL_PICKUP_LAYER -> DRIVEN_PHOTONIC_OR_THERMOELECTRIC_STAGE -> HEAT_DUMP.

No heat-extraction architecture is complete without a declared heat sink, allowed temperature gradient, transport loss model, and maximum accepted heat load.

## 5.9 SPECTRAL_HEAT_EXTRACTION_ARCHITECTURE
The spectral heat-extraction architecture assigns electromagnetic bands by function:
- microwave: activation, calibration, or controlled heating;
- millimeter wave: dielectric-state probing or shallow interrogation;
- terahertz: collective-mode probing and low-energy mode coupling;
- far infrared: infrared-active phonon coupling;
- mid infrared: primary phonon-polariton, radiative, or thermal-emission heat-extraction lane;
- near infrared: fiber readout, Raman interrogation, laser pump, or reference channel;
- visible: laboratory readout, alignment, or surface diagnostics;
- ultraviolet: defect or surface diagnostic only unless qualified otherwise;
- X-ray/gamma: inspection only, not a heat-extraction lane.

For the project heat-removal goal, mid-infrared and far-infrared lanes are primary. Near-infrared and visible lanes are readout and control lanes unless a separate absorption or pumping model is qualified.

## 5.10 FIELD_ASSISTED_HEAT_TRANSPORT_ARCHITECTURE

- field source or ambient field condition;
- coupling interface;
- field-responsive material layer;
- declared carrier layer;
- thermal pickup or receiver interface;
- heat sink, radiator, converter, coolant interface, or pump dump;
- control input when active pumping or modulation is used;
- diagnostic readout channel;
- spacing, alignment, and survivability constraints.

Permitted field-assisted forms include:
- RF telemetry layer coupled to embedded witness nodes;
- microwave magnetic-resonance control layer;
- magnon-assisted spin-caloritronic thermal-state layer;
- near-field electromagnetic heat-transfer stack;
- phonon-polariton electromagnetic coupling layer;
- thermophotovoltaic receiver layer;
- time-modulated photonic heat-pump stage;
- fluctuation-mediated quantum thermal channel between controlled material bodies;
- spacing-managed near-field spacing with Casimir-adjacent mechanical controls.

Preferred field-assisted stack:
HOT_ZONE -> FIELD_COUPLING_LAYER -> DECLARED_CARRIER_LAYER -> RECEIVER_OR_PUMP_STAGE -> HEAT_DUMP.

Field-assisted architecture shall not be accepted unless it distinguishes control energy, transported heat, parasitic heating, and net removed heat.


## 5.11 INTERNAL_LASING_ARCHITECTURE
An internal lasing subsystem is optional. It shall not be assumed from the base HPCS material.

A conformant internal lasing subsystem shall contain:
- gain medium;
- pump path;
- feedback mechanism;
- threshold rule;
- output path;
- residual-heat dump;
- loss budget;
- pressure and temperature survivability limits.

Permitted internal lasing forms are:
- embedded gain inclusion;
- thin-film gain island;
- rare-earth-doped ceramic grain;
- Raman-active crystal region;
- waveguide or fiber-tip gain region;
- random scattering composite;
- polariton heterostructure;
- protected microcavity layer.

The internal laser path is:

pump source -> gain medium -> feedback mechanism -> threshold crossing -> internal emission -> output path -> residual-heat dump -> emission record.

## 5.12 INTERNAL_LASING_FAMILIES
LASER_FAMILY_001_SOLID_STATE_RAMAN_LASER:
  Raman-active solid converts pump photons into coherent Stokes output through stimulated Raman scattering.

LASER_FAMILY_002_RANDOM_LASER_COMPOSITE:
  Gain-doped scattering medium produces laser-like emission through multiple-scattering feedback.

LASER_FAMILY_003_RARE_EARTH_CERAMIC_MICROCAVITY:
  Rare-earth-doped oxide or ceramic inclusion emits through a microcavity, waveguide resonance, or resonant grain-boundary path.

LASER_FAMILY_004_SEMICONDUCTOR_OR_QUANTUM_WELL_GAIN_LAYER:
  Protected semiconductor or quantum-well subsystem emits under declared pump, pressure, and thermal constraints.

LASER_FAMILY_005_PHONON_POLARITON_COHERENT_EMITTER_CANDIDATE:
  Polar material or heterostructure emits coherent THz or IR polariton radiation only after gain, coupling, resonance, coherence, and output proof.

LASER_FAMILY_006_THERMAL_THRESHOLD_OPTICAL_BEACON:
  Subsystem emits when local heat, pressure, strain, damage, or exposure history crosses a declared threshold.

## 5.13 SEPARATION_OF_LASING_FROM_HEAT_EXTRACTION
Internal lasing shall not replace the heat-extraction model.

Laser output may be counted as removed heat only when:
- optical energy exits the protected zone or reaches a declared receiver outside it;
- pump and readout parasitics are debited;
- residual internal laser heat is debited;
- transient stored-energy release is debited;
- the protected-zone baseline comparison remains valid.

Otherwise, internal lasing shall be classified as sensing, beaconing, amplification, or conversion rather than heat extraction.


## 5.14 THERMAL_MODE_TRAP_STORAGE_ARCHITECTURE
[CANDIDATE_DEVICE_BRANCH] Thermal-mode trap storage is a proposed architecture for storing thermal energy in a controlled material state rather than immediately exporting it. This branch is not deployment-qualified by definition.

[ENGINEERING_STANDARD_RULE] The architecture record shall declare:
- heat source region;
- capture layer;
- selected mode or material state;
- anti-thermalization barrier;
- storage reservoir;
- passive leakage path;
- controlled release trigger;
- useful output channel;
- overtemperature and overpressure relief path;
- storage-state readout;
- proof vector for each claimed capture, hold, and release behavior.

[THEORY_ONLY_BRANCH until measured] The candidate chain is:
HEAT_INPUT -> CAPTURE_MODE -> SELECTED_MODE -> STORAGE_STATE -> HOLD_INTERVAL -> RELEASE_TRIGGER -> USEFUL_OUTPUT.

[ENGINEERING_STANDARD_RULE] The virtual cavity shall not be treated as the battery by default. It may be classified only as SELECTOR, GATE, BUFFER, FEEDBACK_PATH, or RELEASE_COUPLER until a measured retention-and-release proof vector promotes it.

[REQUIRES_MATERIAL_QUALIFICATION] The material storage state is the only branch element that may receive battery-like classification, and only after measured E_stored, tau_hold, R_hold, eta_release, safety margin, and cycle behavior pass the declared thresholds.

[REQUIRES_TEST] Permitted trap-storage modes are candidate modes, not deployment claims:
- TMS_MODE_001_LATENT_HEAT_PCM_STORAGE;
- TMS_MODE_002_CERAMIC_ENCAPSULATED_METAL_STORAGE;

- TMS_MODE_007_TRIGGERED_IR_RELEASE;
- TMS_MODE_008_TRIGGERED_COHERENT_LIGHT_RELEASE;
- TMS_MODE_009_TPV_OR_ELECTRICAL_RELEASE_ASSIST.

[UNPROVEN / REQUIRES_TEST] A pure optical or polaritonic trap shall remain classified as SHORT_DURATION_BUFFER unless the same material, geometry, operating regime, and measurement envelope demonstrate a hold interval and useful released energy appropriate to the declared application.

[UNPROVEN / REQUIRES_TEST] Long-duration storage shall not be assigned to optical field energy alone unless measured retention, useful release, and energy accounting meet the application target. Otherwise the claim shall remain buffer, selector, or release-coupler behavior for the declared envelope.

# 6. INTERFACE_DEFINITIONS
## 6.1 HOST_TO_INCLUSION_INTERFACE
The host-to-inclusion interface transfers pressure, strain, temperature, and damage state from the host component to the active inclusion.

Required interface properties:
- mechanical load transfer shall be known or bounded;
- thermal lag shall be known or bounded;
- chemical diffusion shall be below the allowed drift threshold;
- interfacial cracking shall be detectable or shall not corrupt accepted measurements;
- inclusion placement shall not unacceptably weaken the host component.

## 6.2 OPTICAL_INTERFACE
The optical interface transfers photons into and out of the active inclusion.

Required optical properties:
- excitation wavelength shall be compatible with the host, barrier, and inclusion;
- fluorescence background shall remain below the measurement proof/disproof threshold;
- optical attenuation shall remain within the readout budget;
- polarization state shall be preserved when chiral or signed readout is required;
- hot-surface glow shall be separated from the measurement channel when high-temperature interrogation is used.

## 6.3 INFRARED_OPTICAL_INTERFACE
The infrared optical interface shall define:
- excitation wavelength;
- laser linewidth and wavelength stability;
- maximum continuous and pulsed power at the active inclusion;
- spot size or mode-field diameter;
- optical path material;
- return-path geometry;
- background sources;
- filter stack;
- detector spectral range;
- reference channel;
- polarization preservation when signed readout is required.

Infrared delivery shall not be accepted unless the path survives the target pressure, temperature, thermal gradient, vibration, and chemical adjacency. When silica fiber is outside its qualified temperature range, the design shall use remote placement, protective routing, specialty fiber, sapphire-compatible optical paths, or window/standoff coupling.

## 6.4 HEAT_EXTRACTION_INTERFACE
The heat-extraction interface shall define:
- protected thermal region;
- unmodified host baseline;
- hot-side temperature and heat flux;
- thermal pickup material;
- named heat transport carrier;
- transport geometry and length;
- transport attenuation or thermal resistance;
- cold-side sink, radiator, coolant, converter, or pump dump;
- exported heat q_exported;
- protected-zone cooling metric;
- maximum heat load before saturation;
- readout method for heat-path integrity;
- proof/disproof thresholds for delamination, cracking, opacity, or thermal overload.

The interface shall mark as UNPROVEN any heat-extraction claim that lacks a sink or lacks an external work input when heat is moved against the natural thermal gradient. The interface shall mark as DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE any tested claim that improves heat flow inside the local hot region but does not reduce the protected-zone thermal burden relative to the unmodified host baseline.

# 7. DATA_MODELS
## 7.1 ENVIRONMENT_STATE_VECTOR
The environment state vector is:

X_env = {T_op, P_op, epsilon, sigma, D, H, S_opt}

where:
- T_op is operating temperature;
- P_op is operating pressure;
- epsilon is strain;
- sigma is stress state;
- D is damage or disorder index;
- H is exposure-history state;
- S_opt is signed optical-domain state.

## 7.2 OPTICAL_FEATURE_VECTOR
The measured feature vector is:

Y_opt = {omega_i, gamma_i, A_i, Split_i, ROA_i, Phi_i, B_i}

where:
- omega_i is mode frequency;
- gamma_i is mode linewidth;
- A_i is mode amplitude;
- Split_i is mode splitting;
- ROA_i is circular or signed optical contrast;
- Phi_i is phase or fitted complex-response phase when measured;
- B_i is background level.

## 7.3 INFRARED_READOUT_VECTOR
The infrared readout vector is:

Y_IR = {lambda_ex, P_laser, tau_exp, L_path, eta_return, F_bg, G_thermal, Pol_leak, R_ref}

where:
- lambda_ex is excitation wavelength;
- P_laser is power at the active inclusion;
- tau_exp is exposure time or pulse duration;
- L_path is optical path length or equivalent loss model;
- eta_return is return collection efficiency;
- F_bg is fluorescence background;
- G_thermal is thermal-glow background;
- Pol_leak is polarization leakage;
- R_ref is reference-channel response.

## 7.4 CALIBRATION_MODEL
The calibration model is:

X_est = C_inv(Y_opt, M_id, G_inc, H_host, E_env)

where:
- X_est is reconstructed state;
- C_inv is the inverse calibration transform;
- M_id is active material identity;
- G_inc is inclusion geometry;
- H_host is host identity and coupling model;
- E_env is the qualified operating envelope.

## 7.5 PRESSURE_TEMPERATURE_MODE_MODEL
For a single Raman marker mode, the first-order operating model is:

omega_i(T,P,epsilon) = omega_i0 + a_i*(T - T0) + b_i*(P - P0) + c_i*epsilon + k_i*D

where:
- omega_i0 is reference frequency;
- a_i is temperature coefficient;
- b_i is pressure coefficient;
- c_i is strain coefficient;
- k_i is disorder or damage coefficient;
- T0 and P0 are reference conditions.

Higher-order terms may be used only when calibration data supports them.

## 7.6 SIGNED_OPTICAL_RESPONSE_MODEL
When signed optical response is used, the measured contrast is:

ROA_i_obs(T,P,epsilon) = S_opt * ROA_i_mag(T,P,epsilon,D)

where:
- S_opt is in {-1,+1} for a qualified two-domain state;
- ROA_i_mag is nonnegative magnitude after instrument normalization;
- domain mixing may reduce ROA_i_mag without changing the declared sign until the quality gate marks as disproven in envelope the measurement.

## 7.7 EXPOSURE_HISTORY_MODEL
Exposure history may be represented as:

H = f(permanent_peak_shift, irreversible_linewidth_change, retained_split, retained_domain_change)

A witness-mode claim shall require a demonstrated irreversible or hysteretic optical feature. Reversible sensor operation shall not be labeled as exposure-history storage.


## 7.8 HEAT_TRANSPORT_VECTOR
The heat transport vector is:

Y_heat = {CV_protected, A_boundary, V_protected, T_hot, T_sink, T_protected_base, T_protected_active, q_source, q_removed_hot_side, q_boundary_out, q_exported, q_parasitic_internal, q_parasitic_external, q_readout_internal, q_readout_external, q_laser_residual_internal, q_laser_residual_external, q_stored, U_stored, dU_dt, q_storage_release, q_storage_transient_debit, carrier_id, alpha_path, L_heat, eta_heat_passive, q_net_protected, q_net_protected_sustained, q_net_device, heat_burden_coverage, W_in, COP_protected, COP_device, Delta_T_protected, S_sat, H_path}

where:
- CV_protected identifies the protected control volume;
- A_boundary identifies the control-volume boundary;
- V_protected defines the protected volume;
- T_hot is hot-side temperature;
- T_sink is sink, radiator, converter, coolant, or dump-side temperature;
- T_protected_base is protected-region temperature in the unmodified host baseline;
- T_protected_active is protected-region temperature with the HPCS path active;
- q_source is heat generated or entering inside CV_protected;
- q_removed_hot_side is heat removed at the protected-zone boundary or hot-side pickup interface;
- q_boundary_out is gross heat crossing out of CV_protected before debit terms are applied;
- q_exported is heat delivered to a sink or dump outside the protected region;
- q_parasitic_internal is non-readout parasitic heat deposited inside CV_protected;
- q_parasitic_external is non-readout parasitic heat deposited outside CV_protected;
- q_readout_internal is heat deposited inside CV_protected by measurement, reference, pump-leakage, or readout channels;
- q_readout_external is heat deposited outside CV_protected by measurement, reference, pump-leakage, or readout channels;
- q_laser_residual_internal is residual internal heat from an internal-lasing subsystem;
- q_laser_residual_external is residual external heat from an internal-lasing subsystem;
- q_stored and U_stored are stored thermal energy terms in J;
- dU_dt is the time rate of internal-energy storage in CV_protected;
- q_storage_release = max(-dU_dt,0) is storage-release debit;
- q_storage_transient_debit = max(dU_dt,0) + q_storage_release is a conservative transient-storage debit used when accepting sustained heat removal;
- carrier_id identifies the dominant heat transport carrier;
- alpha_path is attenuation or effective loss coefficient;
- L_heat is transport path length;
- eta_heat_passive is passive heat transport efficiency based on q_exported/q_source and shall not be used for active pumping;
- q_net_protected is instantaneous protected-zone net removal as defined only by Section 7.15;
- q_net_protected_sustained is the accepted steady-window value from Section 7.15;
- q_net_device is whole-device net thermal benefit from Section 7.15;
- heat_burden_coverage is q_net_protected_sustained/q_source;
- W_in is external work input when active pumping is claimed;
- COP_protected and COP_device are dimensionless active-pump performance metrics;
- Delta_T_protected = T_protected_base - T_protected_active;
- S_sat is saturation state;
- H_path is heat-path integrity state.

Heat vector units are mandatory: q_source, q_removed_hot_side, q_boundary_out, q_exported, q_parasitic_internal, q_parasitic_external, q_readout_internal, q_readout_external, q_laser_residual_internal, q_laser_residual_external, q_storage_release, q_storage_transient_debit, q_net_protected, q_net_protected_sustained, q_net_device, W_in, and dU_dt are W; q_stored and U_stored are J; temperatures are K unless a conversion is declared; eta_heat_passive, COP_protected, COP_device, and heat_burden_coverage are dimensionless.

## 7.10 HEAT_EXTRACTION_MODE_MODEL
This section defines the operating summary for heat-extraction modes. Section 7.15 is the single normative source for protected-zone heat accounting, q_net_protected, q_net_protected_sustained, q_net_device, heat_burden_coverage, COP_protected, and COP_device.

Passive heat extraction shall satisfy:

T_hot > T_sink

and shall report:

eta_heat_passive = q_exported / q_source

The protected-zone cooling metric is:

Delta_T_protected = T_protected_base - T_protected_active

A passive heat-extraction claim requires Delta_T_protected > 0 under baseline-equivalent operating load and shall also demonstrate that transient storage is not being mistaken for extraction.

The protected control-volume balance shall be closed with the Section 7.15 term definitions. During transient operation, the claim shall report dU_dt, U_stored, q_storage_release, and q_storage_transient_debit. A falling local temperature caused only by heat storage in a nearby layer shall be classified as transient redistribution until a sink/export path is verified.

A claim that only empties stored energy from CV_protected without covering the continuing source load shall be marked DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE as transient discharge, not heat extraction.

Assisted heat pumping shall report external work input W_in and split coefficient of performance into protected-region and device-level forms:

COP_protected = q_net_protected_sustained / W_in

COP_device = q_net_device / W_in

A declared heat pump shall identify whether q_removed_hot_side is measured at the protected-zone boundary, hot-side pickup, carrier path, or cold-side dump. A passive heat highway shall not be reported as active pumping.

Y_laser = {laser_mode_id, laser_family, gain_medium_id, pump_class, pump_band, output_band, feedback_type, threshold_status, P_pump_abs, P_threshold, G_net, L_total, P_out_laser, P_out_laser_min, q_laser_residual_internal, q_laser_residual_external, q_laser_residual_internal_max, q_laser_residual_external_max, S_laser}

where:

- f_field is the operating frequency or equivalent optical frequency;
- P_field is the applied field power or bounded ambient field level;

- q_parasitic_external is heat added outside the protected region;
- q_readout_internal and q_readout_external are measurement-induced heat terms assigned by deposition location;

- q_net_protected_sustained is accepted sustained protected-zone net removal;
- heat_burden_coverage is q_net_protected_sustained/q_source;

## 7.15 NET_HEAT_REMOVAL_MODEL
Section 7.15 is the single normative heat-accounting source for heat-extraction, field-assisted cooling, active heat-pump, and internal-lasing thermal claims.

Readout heat naming rule:
- q_readout_internal is readout, reference, probe, pump-leakage, or detector-adjacent heat deposited inside CV_protected;
- q_readout_external is readout, reference, probe, pump-leakage, or detector-adjacent heat deposited outside CV_protected;
- generic q_readout may appear only as an informal aggregate and shall not be used in acceptance equations.

Control-volume balance for CV_protected is:

dU_dt = q_source + q_parasitic_internal + q_readout_internal + q_laser_residual_internal - q_removed_hot_side

Storage-release debit is:

q_storage_release = max(-dU_dt,0)

Conservative transient-storage debit is:

q_storage_transient_debit = max(dU_dt,0) + q_storage_release

This debit intentionally treats both storage accumulation and storage release as acceptance penalties. It prevents temporary cooling, local heat shuffling, or release of previously stored energy from being misclassified as sustained heat extraction.

Instantaneous protected-zone net removal is:

q_net_protected = q_removed_hot_side - q_parasitic_internal - q_readout_internal - q_laser_residual_internal - q_storage_transient_debit

Accepted sustained protected-zone net removal is the steady-window average:

q_net_protected_sustained = average(q_net_protected) over the steady-state acceptance window

Whole-device net thermal benefit may also report:

q_net_device = q_exported - q_parasitic_internal - q_parasitic_external - q_readout_internal - q_readout_external - q_laser_residual_internal - q_laser_residual_external

Heat burden coverage shall be reported as:

heat_burden_coverage = q_net_protected_sustained / q_source

Protected-region active-pump performance shall be reported as:

COP_protected = q_net_protected_sustained / W_in

Device-level active-pump performance shall be reported as:

COP_device = q_net_device / W_in

where q_removed_hot_side is heat removed across the protected boundary, q_exported is heat delivered to a declared receiver or dump, q_parasitic_internal is heat added inside the protected region, q_parasitic_external is heat generated outside the protected region, q_readout_internal/external are readout heat terms assigned by deposition location, q_laser_residual_internal/external are residual heat terms from internal-lasing operation, and q_storage_transient_debit prevents transient storage behavior from being counted as sustained source-load removal.

A field-assisted heat pump is accepted only when q_net_protected_sustained > 0, heat_burden_coverage > 0, Delta_T_protected > 0, and the control-volume balance is closed under the declared operating envelope. q_net_device may be lower than q_net_protected_sustained when external drive electronics or converter losses are counted.

A reported cooling effect shall be marked DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE or reclassified when q_stored, dU_dt, q_storage_release, or q_storage_transient_debit explains the protected-zone temperature drop without verified export, dump, or sustained boundary heat flow.


## 7.16 NEAR_FIELD_HEAT_MODEL
For near-field electromagnetic transfer, the minimum declared variables are:

Y_nf = {T_hot, T_recv, d_spacing, d_spacing_worst, d_nf_max, M_nf, M_nf_worst, material_hot, material_recv, A_eff, q_nf, q_leak, S_spacing}

where:
- T_hot is hot-side temperature;
- T_recv is receiver or dump-side temperature;
- d_spacing is nominal qualified spacing or effective near-field spacing;
- d_spacing_worst is the maximum effective spacing across roughness, vibration, thermal expansion, pressure deformation, contamination, and service drift;
- d_nf_max is the declared maximum spacing for the qualified near-field or modal-decay envelope;
- M_nf = d_nf_max - d_spacing and shall remain positive during nominal operation;
- M_nf_worst = d_nf_max - d_spacing_worst and shall remain positive for noncontact near-field acceptance;
- material_hot and material_recv identify both optical-response surfaces;
- A_eff is effective coupling area;
- q_nf is measured or bounded near-field heat flux;
- q_leak is parasitic conductive, convective, or far-field leakage;
- S_spacing is spacing integrity state.

Near-field heat transfer shall not be collapsed into ordinary conduction unless the mechanism has been reclassified as contact conduction.

Near-field qualification shall declare one formal spacing rule:
- d_spacing <= lambda_peak/(2*pi*N_margin) for evanescent electromagnetic operation;
- d_spacing <= delta_modal for modal-decay-length operation; or
- contact-coupled operation, in which the mechanism is not claimed as noncontact near-field transfer.

N_margin and delta_modal shall be measured or bounded for the selected material pair, temperature, pressure, roughness, and vibration state. d_spacing_worst and M_nf_worst shall be reported, and noncontact near-field acceptance shall require M_nf_worst > 0.

## 7.17 FIELD_MODE_CLASSIFICATION_MODEL
Field-assisted modes shall be classified before acceptance:

MODE_CLASS = {SUPPORT_CONTROL, PASSIVE_TRANSPORT, CONVERSION, ACTIVE_CONTROL, ACTIVE_PUMP_CANDIDATE, ACTIVE_PUMP}

where:
- SUPPORT_CONTROL covers telemetry, modulation, interrogation, and calibration without a direct heat-removal claim;
- PASSIVE_TRANSPORT covers heat movement down a natural gradient through a declared carrier;
- CONVERSION covers thermal photon, magnonic, thermoelectric, thermophotovoltaic, or optical conversion with residual heat handling;
- ACTIVE_CONTROL covers externally driven modulation that changes heat-flow conductance or routing without claiming heat pumping against a gradient;
- ACTIVE_PUMP_CANDIDATE covers time-modulated or work-driven branches proposed as pumps before full heat-balance proof;
- ACTIVE_PUMP covers work-driven heat movement that can exceed or oppose passive heat flow and must declare W_in, modulation law, COP, sink, and q_net_protected.

RF modes default to SUPPORT_CONTROL. Microwave dielectric modes default to SUPPORT_CONTROL or calibration heating. Near-field electromagnetic modes default to PASSIVE_TRANSPORT unless time modulation, bias, or converter work input is declared. Time-modulated photonic heat-pump modes default to ACTIVE_PUMP_CANDIDATE until W_in, modulation law, sink, q_net_protected, Delta_T_protected, and closed control-volume balance are qualified. After qualification they may be promoted to ACTIVE_PUMP.

## 7.18 TIME_MODULATION_MODEL
A time-modulated photonic or electromagnetic heat-pump branch shall declare:

M(t) = M_0 + Delta_M * f_mod(t, Omega_mod, phi_mod, duty_mod)

where:
- M(t) is the modulated material, optical, electromagnetic, or boundary property;
- M_0 is the unbiased property;
- Delta_M is modulation amplitude;
- f_mod is the bounded waveform;
- Omega_mod is modulation angular frequency;
- phi_mod is modulation phase;
- duty_mod is duty cycle when pulsed operation is used.

The modulation law shall be tied to W_in, thermal response time, receiver dump, q_net_protected_sustained, and heat_burden_coverage. It shall declare A_mod, f_mod, D_mod, q_drive_heat, q_drive_heat_internal, q_drive_heat_external, and stability bounds. A time-modulated branch that does not reduce protected-zone burden after drive heat is debited shall be classified as ACTIVE_CONTROL, diagnostic modulation, or marked DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE. Runaway oscillation, increasing self-heating, loss of sink capacity, or q_drive_heat_internal greater than the removed protected heat shall mark as disproven in envelope ACTIVE_PUMP promotion.

TRUTH_CLASS = {SUPPORTED_PHYSICS, ENGINEERING_STANDARD_RULE, CANDIDATE_DEVICE_BRANCH, THEORY_ONLY_BRANCH, REQUIRES_MATERIAL_QUALIFICATION, UNPROVEN, PROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE, DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE, INDETERMINATE, NEEDS_DISCRIMINATING_TEST}

Y_truth = {
  claim_id,
  branch_id,
  truth_class,
  material_readiness_tag,
  operating_regime_tag,

Default assignment rules:
- Raman sensing, Stokes/anti-Stokes thermometry, and fiber optical readout default to SUPPORTED_PHYSICS with PROVEN_BASIS, but harsh embedded use remains REQUIRES_MATERIAL_QUALIFICATION until the selected host and inclusion pass the screening gates.
- Passive solid-state heat conduction and radiative heat dumping default to SUPPORTED_PHYSICS plus ENGINEERING_STANDARD_RULE for the accounting model.
- Phonon-polariton heat transport defaults to SUPPORTED_PHYSICS for the carrier mechanism and CANDIDATE_DEVICE_BRANCH for this project-specific embedded heat-highway construction.
- Near-field electromagnetic heat transfer defaults to SUPPORTED_PHYSICS for the mechanism and REQUIRES_MATERIAL_QUALIFICATION for any high-pressure/high-temperature embedded stack.
- Time-modulated photonic heat pumping defaults to THEORY_ONLY_BRANCH or CANDIDATE_DEVICE_BRANCH until closed control-volume heat-pump proof is supplied.
- Magnon-assisted transport defaults to SUPPORTED_PHYSICS for spin-caloritronic mechanisms and REQUIRES_MATERIAL_QUALIFICATION for any high-temperature or high-pressure use.

- Thermal-threshold optical beacons default to CANDIDATE_DEVICE_BRANCH and require residual-heat debit and failure masking checks.

Promotion rule:
A branch may move toward DEPLOYMENT_QUALIFIED only when its proof vector, material screening vector, operating-regime tag, heat or optical accounting, and safety gates all pass for the declared application.

## 7.21 SUPPORTING_MATH_MODEL
The following equations define the minimum supporting math set for material screening, heat transport, optical readout, field coupling, and internal lasing.

Steady conduction through a layer:
q_cond = k*A*(T_hot - T_cold)/L
R_th = L/(k*A)
Delta_T_layer = q_cond*R_th

Thermal diffusion time:
tau_diff = L^2/alpha_th
alpha_th = k/(rho*C_p)

Lumped thermal balance:
C_th*dT/dt = q_in - q_out - q_stored_exported

Radiative exchange upper-level model:
q_rad = epsilon*sigma*A*(T_hot^4 - T_cold^4)

Spectral radiative branch weighting:
q_rad_band = integral_{lambda_1}^{lambda_2} epsilon(lambda,T)*M_bb(lambda,T)*A*d_lambda

Transport attenuation:
I_out = I_in*exp(-alpha_path*L_path)
eta_transport = P_out/P_in

Phonon-polariton route condition:
|omega_source - omega_mode| <= Gamma_eff
Q_mode = omega_mode/Gamma_eff
L_prop = 1/(2*Im(k_mode))

Near-field spacing guard:
M_nf = d_limit/d_spacing_worst
accept_near_field = TRUE only if M_nf >= 1 and S_spacing == PASS

Raman temperature ratio:
I_AS/I_S = C_cal*exp(-h*c*nu_R/(k_B*T))
T = (h*c*nu_R/k_B)/ln(C_cal*I_S/I_AS)

Raman pressure/strain feature model:
Delta_nu_i = a_i*Delta_T + b_i*Delta_P + c_i*epsilon_mech + d_i*damage_index

Optical readout heating:
q_readout_internal = P_probe_abs_internal + P_pump_abs_internal - P_optical_escape_from_protected
q_readout_external = P_probe_abs_external + P_pump_abs_external

Internal lasing threshold model:
P_pump_abs >= P_threshold
G_net > L_total
P_out_laser >= P_out_laser_min
q_laser_residual_internal <= q_laser_residual_internal_max

Heat burden coverage:
heat_burden_coverage = q_net_protected_sustained/q_source

Active pump coefficients:
COP_protected = q_net_protected_sustained/W_in
COP_device = q_removed_to_sink/W_in

Material promotion score:
M_score = w_surv*M_survivability + w_host*M_host_compatibility + w_carrier*M_carrier_support + w_loss*M_low_loss + w_readout*M_readout_quality + w_sink*M_sink_interface + w_cycle*M_cycling_stability

All math symbols shall be unit-typed in the proof record. Any equation used outside its valid geometry, temperature range, pressure range, or optical band shall be tagged REQUIRES_MATERIAL_QUALIFICATION or DISPROVE_IF_UNPROVEN.

## 7.23 THERMAL_MODE_TRAP_STORAGE_MODEL
[ENGINEERING_STANDARD_RULE] Thermal-mode trap storage shall be represented by TRAP_STORAGE_RECORD before any storage claim is evaluated.

  truth_class,
  material_readiness_tag,
  operating_regime_tag,
  proof_vector_id,
  test_envelope_id,
  qualification_status,
  heat_source_region,
  capture_mode,
  trap_type,
  storage_state_type,
  anti_thermalization_barrier,
  virtual_cavity_role,
  E_in,
  E_stored,
  E_lost,
  E_useful_out,
  E_remaining,
  E_damage,
  P_capture,
  P_leak_to_heat,
  P_release_useful,
  tau_hold,
  tau_required,
  R_hold,
  eta_capture,

}

[ENGINEERING_STANDARD_RULE] Storage equations are accounting equations. They do not prove storage unless their terms are measured, bounded, and tied to the same test envelope.

P_capture = eta_capture * q_heat_in

dE_store/dt = P_capture - P_leak_to_heat - P_release_useful

tau_hold = E_stored / max(P_leak_to_heat, epsilon_loss)

R_hold(t) = E_stored(t) / E_stored(0)

eta_release = E_useful_out / max(E_stored, epsilon_energy)

E_balance_error = E_in - E_stored - E_lost - E_useful_out - E_remaining

[REQUIRES_TEST] Acceptance inequalities are test gates, not assumed material behavior:

capture_rate_condition: P_capture > P_leak_to_heat during charge interval

release_condition: P_release_useful > P_leak_to_heat during commanded discharge interval

hold_condition: tau_hold >= tau_required

retention_condition: R_hold(tau_required) >= R_hold_min

safety_energy_condition: E_stored < E_damage

safety_temperature_condition: T_local_max < T_surv

safety_pressure_condition: P_internal_max < P_limit

[DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE] A storage claim shall be marked disproven for the declared envelope if E_balance_error exceeds declared uncertainty, if useful release is not declared, if uncontrolled leakage dominates the commanded release path, or if the measured proof vector is not tied to the same material, geometry, host, and operating regime.

- STORAGE_METASTABLE_PHASE;
- STORAGE_TRAPPED_CHARGE;
- STORAGE_DEFECT_EXCITATION;
- STORAGE_STRAIN_STATE;
- STORAGE_DOMAIN_STATE;
- STORAGE_SPIN_STATE;
- STORAGE_CHEMICAL_POTENTIAL;
- STORAGE_OPTICAL_POLARITON_BUFFER.

[UNPROVEN / REQUIRES_TEST] STORAGE_OPTICAL_POLARITON_BUFFER shall not be promoted to long-duration battery behavior unless measured hold interval, retention ratio, release efficiency, and safety margin satisfy the declared application target.

# 8. ALGORITHMIC_FORMALISM
## 8.1 STATE_RECONSTRUCTION_ALGORITHM
The canonical reconstruction procedure is:
1. acquire raw spectrum and instrument metadata;
2. subtract bounded background;
3. identify qualified marker modes;
4. fit omega_i, gamma_i, A_i, Split_i, and ROA_i when available;
5. apply thermal, pressure, strain, and damage calibration coefficients;
6. evaluate measurement quality gates;
7. compute X_est and U_bound;
8. return the state vector or mark as disproven in envelope the measurement.

## 8.2 INFRARED_OPERATION_MODES
The infrared branch defines the following operating modes:
- IR_MODE_001_RAMAN_SHIFT: pressure, strain, or phase-state inference from Raman peak shift;
- IR_MODE_002_RAMAN_LINEWIDTH: damage, disorder, thermal degradation, or coupling loss inference from linewidth growth;
- IR_MODE_003_STOKES_ANTISTOKES: temperature inference from Stokes/anti-Stokes intensity ratio;
- IR_MODE_004_SIGNED_POLARIZATION: domain or chiral-state inference from opposite polarization or circular channels;
- IR_MODE_005_ABSORPTION_TRANSMISSION: process-state or material-state inference from absorption, transmission, or reflection changes;
- IR_MODE_006_DISTRIBUTED_BACKSCATTER: thermal mapping along a fiber path using distributed optical backscatter;
- IR_MODE_007_REFERENCE_DRIFT: correction using a reference line, reference inclusion, or stable return channel.

Each operating mode shall declare whether it is a live sensor mode, retained witness mode, identity/provenance mode, or proof-status-only health check.

## 8.3 IR_POWER_AND_HEATING_CONTROL
Laser interrogation shall preserve the measured state. The local laser load shall satisfy:

DeltaT_laser <= DeltaT_allowed

where DeltaT_laser is laser-induced local heating and DeltaT_allowed is the maximum readout-induced temperature error allowed by the calibration envelope.

The power-control procedure is:
1. start below the qualified power level;
2. acquire reference background;
3. acquire the marker response;
4. test for power-dependent peak shift, linewidth growth, or irreversible change;
5. reduce power or mark as disproven in envelope the measurement if laser-induced drift exceeds threshold.

## 8.4 STOKES_ANTISTOKES_TEMPERATURE_MODEL
When Stokes/anti-Stokes temperature readout is used, the accepted model shall include a calibrated instrument factor:

I_AS/I_S = K_inst * exp(-h*c*nu_m/(kB*T))

where:
- I_AS is anti-Stokes intensity;
- I_S is Stokes intensity;
- K_inst is the calibrated instrument and collection factor;
- nu_m is the Raman shift in cm^-1;
- h*c*nu_m converts wavenumber to energy;
- kB is Boltzmann constant;
- T is absolute temperature.

The implementation shall not use the ratio as an absolute thermometer unless detector response, filter transmission, path loss, and wavelength-dependent collection are calibrated.

## 8.5 QUALITY_GATE
A measurement shall be marked DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE when any required condition fails:
- marker mode not detected;
- peak fit residual exceeds threshold;
- background exceeds threshold;
- optical path attenuation exceeds threshold;
- polarization leakage exceeds threshold for signed response;
- inferred state lies outside the qualified envelope;
- host/inclusion coupling condition is invalid;
- thermal glow or fluorescence dominates the signal.

## 8.6 PRESSURE_STRAIN_SEPARATION
Pressure and strain shall not be treated as identical when the host imposes anisotropic loading.

The minimum separation rule is:
- hydrostatic calibration may be used for pressure-cell-style applications;
- nonhydrostatic host loading requires at least one anisotropy-sensitive feature or a host mechanics model;
- mode splitting shall be treated as evidence of anisotropic stress or symmetry lowering, not as scalar pressure alone.

## 8.7 TEMPERATURE_COMPENSATION
Temperature compensation shall use one of the following methods:
- independent temperature measurement;
- dual-mode optical calibration with different pressure and temperature coefficients;
- reference inclusion with weak pressure coefficient;
- time-separated calibration when pressure is constant;
- qualified thermal model of the host and inclusion.

A pressure estimate shall not be accepted from a single thermally sensitive mode unless temperature is independently bounded.

## 8.8 DAMAGE_INDEX
The damage or disorder index may be computed from normalized linewidth growth, amplitude loss, irreversible peak shift, or mode disappearance.

A minimum model is:

D = clamp((gamma_i - gamma_i_ref)/gamma_i_ref, 0, D_max)

D is a diagnostic indicator unless separately correlated with physical crack length, porosity, dislocation density, or fatigue life.

## 8.9 DOMAIN_STATE_OPERATION
If domain readout is used, the optical sign shall be handled as:

S_opt = sign(ROA_i_obs)

only after correction for instrument handedness and polarization leakage.

Domain readout shall be marked DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE when:
- multiple domains average to near-zero contrast;
- optical focus spans multiple domain regions without a mixing model;
- the material has crossed a domain-randomizing thermal or pressure boundary;
- the optical channel is not polarization-calibrated.

3. assign the field mode class;
4. identify heat source and protected region;
5. identify the unmodified host baseline;
6. identify heat sink, receiver, converter, radiator, coolant interface, or pump dump;

[ENGINEERING_STANDARD_RULE] Operation steps:

## 8.25 CTI_HPCS_PROOF_STATUS_ALGORITHM
For any HPCS claim with unsettled or competing explanations, CTI shall assist by scoring hypotheses rather than deleting branches.

Candidate statuses are:
- UNPROVEN;
- PROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE;
- DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE;
- INDETERMINATE;
- NEEDS_DISCRIMINATING_TEST.

The CTI_HPCS_PROOF_RECORD shall bind every proof or disproof to:
1. material_stack_id;
2. geometry_id;
3. test_envelope_id;
4. baseline_id;
5. operating_regime;
6. measurement_method;
7. uncertainty_bound;
8. evidence_inputs;
9. next_discriminating_test when the result remains unsettled.

Default CTI scoring drivers are:
- D1 evidence_quality;
- D2 energy_balance_closure;
- D3 repeatability;
- D4 baseline_separation;
- D5 mechanism_specificity;
- D6 test_envelope_match;
- D7 measurement_uncertainty_penalty;
- D8 falsification_strength;
- D9 safety_margin;
- D10 readiness_for_next_build.

Default scoring surface:
cti_score = 5*D1 + 5*D2 + 4*D3 + 4*D4 + 3*D5 + 3*D6 - 4*D7 + 4*D8 + 3*D9 + 2*D10

If one hypothesis exceeds all alternatives by the declared margin and passes the required proof vector, the claim status may become PROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE or DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE inside that envelope only.

If two or more hypotheses remain within the declared margin, the claim status shall be NEEDS_DISCRIMINATING_TEST and the next test shall be recorded.

If evidence is incomplete, outside envelope, or not tied to the same material stack, the claim status shall be INDETERMINATE.

# 10. CONSTRAINTS
The system shall obey the following constraints:
- the active inclusion shall remain solid-state during qualified operation;
- the active inclusion shall not chemically poison or weaken the host component beyond design limits;
- the active inclusion shall not require electrical feedthroughs for passive operation;
- the readout path shall not require destructive sectioning unless the application is post-event forensic inspection;
- pressure, temperature, strain, and damage shall not be collapsed into a single scalar unless the application only requires a threshold indicator;
- chiral or signed response shall not be claimed unless opposite-channel calibration is performed;
- permanent witness behavior shall not be claimed from reversible mode shifts alone;
- material selection shall prioritize survivability before chiral enhancement;
- calibration curves shall include uncertainty bounds;
- operating envelopes shall be explicit;
- infrared laser power shall remain below damage, local-heating, phase-change, and domain-erasure thresholds;
- infrared readout shall include a reference or background channel when used in high-temperature environments;
- wavelength selection shall be justified by a Raman-efficiency, fluorescence, absorption, detector, and optical-path trade study;
- fiber-delivered operation shall qualify the fiber, connector, feedthrough, window, and coating as part of the operating envelope;
- heat extraction shall not be claimed unless a heat sink, radiator, converter, coolant interface, or pump dump is declared;
- active heat pumping shall not be claimed unless external work input and COP are declared;
- phonon-polariton transport shall not be claimed unless the material supports the relevant infrared-active mode and the coupling band is declared;
- microwave operation shall be treated as heating, activation, or calibration unless a qualified extraction path is provided;
- X-ray and gamma operation shall be limited to inspection and shall not be used as a thermal-management lane;
- RF operation shall be treated as telemetry, modulation, or control unless a qualified heat-removal path is declared;
- microwave operation shall separate useful magnetic or magnonic response from ordinary dielectric heating;
- near-field electromagnetic heat transfer shall declare spacing, surface condition, and receiver dump;
- quantum-field terminology shall be limited to fluctuation-mediated thermal transport between material bodies and shall not be used to claim vacuum energy extraction;
- time-modulated photonic heat pumping shall declare work input, modulation law, q_net_protected_sustained, protected-zone cooling metric, and receiver dump;
- thermophotovoltaic conversion shall distinguish converted electrical output from residual dumped heat;
- field-assisted cooling shall require q_net_protected_sustained > 0 and Delta_T_protected > 0 for the protected region against the unmodified host baseline;
- heat-extraction trials shall define CV_protected, boundary geometry, measurement points, and averaging interval before claiming protected-zone cooling;
- transient cooling shall not be accepted as heat extraction unless dU_dt and q_stored are measured or bounded;
- baseline comparison shall use equivalent geometry, heat load, sink condition, ambient state, pressure state, strain state, and measurement window;
- readout-induced heating shall be counted as internal or external parasitic heat according to deposition location;
- thermophotovoltaic conversion shall include a residual heat dump and shall not count converted electrical output as removed heat unless receiver heat is also marked DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE away from the protected region;
- magnon-assisted operation shall require magnetic phase stability margin through the full operating envelope;
- RF operation shall remain SUPPORT_CONTROL unless carrier, sink, and protected-zone cooling metric are separately qualified.

# 12. SECURITY_CONSIDERATIONS
Embedded witness materials can reveal component identity, operating history, overload exposure, or manufacturing provenance. Access to calibration tables and readout methods may therefore expose sensitive operational data.

Security controls shall include:
- calibration record integrity;
- instrument-handness audit for signed response;
- anti-substitution marking for active inclusions;
- provenance records for material batches;
- tamper detection for accessible optical windows or tags;
- separation of public identifier response from protected diagnostic response where required.

The material shall not be treated as a secure identity system unless its optical signature, placement, and calibration chain are protected against substitution.


Trap-storage branches shall be treated as stored-energy hazards. Safety review shall account for delayed release, burst release, pressure buildup, phase expansion, uncontrolled optical emission, host fracture, and thermal runaway.

A high-retention trap shall include a relief or proof-status state. Systems that capture energy without a declared safe release path shall fail conformance.

1. select a high-temperature, pressure-stable Raman-active oxide or ceramic candidate;
2. measure its Raman modes across the target temperature envelope;
3. measure its Raman modes across the target pressure envelope;
4. test host-material chemical compatibility;
5. define inclusion geometry with low host-weakening risk;
6. test optical access through the intended barrier or window;
7. select the infrared wavelength lane and detector class;
8. measure fluorescence, thermal glow, fiber/window background, and host absorption;
9. qualify maximum laser power and exposure time;
10. build first-order calibration coefficients;
11. perform thermal-pressure cycling;
12. qualify proof/disproof thresholds;
13. embed into a noncritical host coupon;
14. compare optical state estimates against external sensors;
15. promote only the validated state variables into application use.

Near-term project configurations:
- fiber-tip witness coating for pressure or thermal exposure;
- ceramic coating marker for hot-zone damage inspection;
- embedded grain array for structural health coupons;
- protected Raman patch for pressure vessel monitoring;
- signed-domain optical tag for component provenance;
- 1064 nm reduced-fluorescence Raman witness marker;
- 1550 nm fiber-network optical health-check lane;

- Stokes/anti-Stokes thermal readout path paired with discrete pressure marker.


Heat-extraction implementation path:
1. identify protected hot zone and allowed maximum temperature;
2. identify available cold-side dump, radiator, coolant interface, converter, or work-driven pump stage;
3. choose passive or assisted heat-extraction mode;
4. select carrier: phonon conduction, phonon-polariton transport, infrared radiation, or assisted thermophotonic/thermoelectric channel;
5. select spectral lane, with mid-IR/far-IR prioritized for heat extraction;
6. define pickup, transport, and dump geometry;
7. measure heat flux, path loss, and saturation threshold;
8. add optical or thermal path-readout channel;
9. test under combined temperature, pressure, strain, and cycling;
10. promote only measured heat-removal capacity into application use.

Near-term heat-extraction project configurations:
- SiC or sapphire-compatible thermal pickup coupon;
- mid-IR radiative dump layer bonded to ceramic host;
- phonon-polariton transport strip between hot spot and colder shell;
- embedded heat-path witness read by near-IR Raman or mid-IR return;
- damage-interruption heat-path coupon for crack/delamination detection;
- passive heat highway paired with external radiator or coolant jacket;
- assisted thermophotonic stage only where external work input and COP can be measured.

Field-assisted implementation path:
1. select the field-assisted branch: RF telemetry, microwave magnetic control, magnonic layer, near-field EM transfer, TPV conversion, or time-modulated photonic pump;
2. declare field band, frequency, and source geometry;
3. identify the coupled carrier and mark as disproven in envelope noncoupled field exposure;
4. measure parasitic heating from the field source;
5. measure q_removed from the protected region and all internal/external parasitic terms;
6. compute q_net_protected and mark as disproven in envelope q_net_protected_sustained <= 0 for cooling claims;
7. qualify spacing or magnetic phase stability when required;
8. attach a heat sink, receiver, converter, radiator, coolant interface, or pump dump;
9. test survivability under combined temperature, pressure, vibration, and cycling;
10. promote only modes with measured carrier coupling and net benefit.

Near-term field-assisted project configurations:
- RF telemetry and modulation layer for embedded witness nodes;
- microwave magnetic-resonance thermal-valve coupon;
- magnonic spin-caloritronic thermal-state readout coupon;
- near-field electromagnetic heat-transfer stack with controlled spacing;
- phonon-polariton layer coupled to a mid-IR field source;
- TPV receiver at the cold-side dump;
- time-modulated photonic heat-pump bench article with explicit work input and heat-flux measurement.


Material heat-extraction screening shall evaluate:
1. maximum operating temperature and pressure margin;
2. chemical compatibility with host and barrier;
3. thermal conductivity and diffusivity;
4. infrared-active phonon band or polariton support;
5. emissivity and spectral selectivity when radiative dumping is used;
6. damping under pressure, strain, and temperature;
7. optical or Raman readout compatibility;
8. manufacturable geometry for pickup, transport, and dump interfaces;
9. degradation under cycling;
10. failure consequence if the material cracks, reacts, or delaminates.

Material selection priority shall be:
1. survivability;
2. host compatibility;
3. repeatable calibration;
4. optical readability;
5. domain stability;
6. signed or chiral enhancement;
7. miniaturization.

Promotion rule:
A material family is not qualified by name. It is promoted only after M_screen is filled and the selected carrier, construction form, host compatibility, calibration path, and proof/disproof trigger are all declared.

Material acceptance and ranking score:

M_score = w_surv*M_survivability + w_host*M_host_compatibility + w_carrier*M_carrier_support + w_loss*M_low_loss + w_readout*M_readout_quality + w_sink*M_sink_interface + w_cycle*M_cycling_stability

where each M_* score is normalized from 0 to 1 and each w_* weight is declared for the application class. Default harsh-environment weighting is:
- w_surv = 0.25;
- w_host = 0.20;
- w_carrier = 0.15;
- w_loss = 0.15;
- w_readout = 0.10;
- w_sink = 0.10;
- w_cycle = 0.05.

Minimum promotion rule:
- mark as disproven in envelope if M_survivability < 0.70;
- mark as disproven in envelope if M_host_compatibility < 0.70;
- mark as disproven in envelope if M_carrier_support < 0.60;
- mark as disproven in envelope if M_sink_interface < 0.50 when heat extraction is claimed;
- promote only if M_score >= 0.75 for deployment candidate status;
- classify 0.60 <= M_score < 0.75 as research candidate;
- classify M_score < 0.60 as marked DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE for the declared application.

## 14.1 PROTOTYPE_LADDER
The prototype ladder converts this standard from a broad material standard into staged construction work. A higher prototype stage shall not be claimed until the prior applicable stage has passed its acceptance gate or has a declared bypass rationale.

| Stage | Name | Goal | Minimum build | Acceptance gate | Promotion output |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| P0 | Bench coupon baseline | Establish room-condition optical and thermal baseline | flat coupon, active material patch or grain, known substrate, external Raman/IR probe | repeatable marker feature and background record | BASELINE_OPTICAL_RECORD |
| P1 | Material inclusion coupon | Test active inclusion inside or on a host material | host coupon plus embedded grain, film island, or coating | marker survives attachment, curing, or bonding | MATERIAL_INCLUSION_RECORD |
| P2 | Optical access coupon | Prove fiber/window/standoff readout | P1 plus sapphire window, fiber, or standoff path | signal quality passes through intended access path | OPTICAL_ACCESS_RECORD |
| P3 | Thermal exposure coupon | Measure temperature response and readout heating | P2 plus controlled hot plate, furnace, or laser-heated zone | calibrated Raman/IR temperature feature and readout heat debit | THERMAL_CALIBRATION_RECORD |
| P4 | Pressure/strain coupon | Measure pressure, strain, or load feature separation | P2/P3 plus pressure cell, press, bending fixture, or strain frame | feature model separates temperature from pressure/strain within uncertainty | PRESSURE_STRAIN_RECORD |
| P5 | Heat-path demonstrator | Test actual heat movement against baseline | hot-zone source, pickup layer, transport layer, sink/dump, reference sensors | q_net_protected_sustained > 0 and heat_burden_coverage declared | HEAT_PATH_DEMO_RECORD |
| P6 | Field-assisted demonstrator | Test RF, microwave, near-field, magnonic, TPV, or time-modulated field branch | P5 plus field source and field-responsive layer | net benefit remains after field parasitic heat and baseline equivalence | FIELD_ASSISTED_RECORD |
| P7 | Internal-lasing candidate coupon | Test thresholded optical emission | gain inclusion, pump path, feedback path, output collector, heat debit | P_out_laser >= P_out_laser_min and residual heat below limit | LASER_CANDIDATE_RECORD |
| P8 | Combined harsh-cycle article | Cycle thermal, pressure, strain, optical, and host compatibility | best prior stack in noncritical host article | calibration remains within drift limit after cycling | HARSH_CYCLE_RECORD |
| P9 | Deployment-qualified stack | Qualify one declared stack for one declared envelope | final stack, final host, final readout, final sink/dump | all required gates pass for declared envelope only | DEPLOYMENT_QUALIFICATION_RECORD |

Prototype ladder guard:
A prototype result applies only to its declared material, geometry, host, optical path, pressure range, temperature range, heat load, sink condition, and measurement interval. A result from one stage shall not be generalized to deployment unless P9 is passed.

## 14.3 TEST_PROTOCOLS
Each test protocol shall emit a named record and a proof-status state when it fails.

TEST_HPCS_001_ROOM_OPTICAL_BASELINE:
- input: material coupon, optical lane, detector settings;
- procedure: collect dark, background, reference, and marker spectra at room condition;
- pass: marker features are repeatable and fit residuals are below threshold;
- output: BASELINE_OPTICAL_RECORD.

TEST_HPCS_002_READOUT_HEATING:
- input: optical lane, probe power series, local temperature reference;
- procedure: measure feature shift versus probe power and dwell time;
- pass: q_readout_internal and DeltaT_laser remain below allowed uncertainty;
- output: READOUT_HEATING_RECORD.

TEST_HPCS_003_THERMAL_CYCLING_SURVIVABILITY:
- input: coupon, target T_min/T_max, cycle count;
- procedure: cycle temperature while measuring feature drift before, during, and after cycling;
- pass: drift, cracking, optical darkening, and host reaction remain below threshold;
- output: THERMAL_CYCLE_RECORD.

TEST_HPCS_004_PRESSURE_STRAIN_CALIBRATION:
- input: coupon, pressure or strain fixture, temperature reference;
- procedure: sweep pressure/strain while controlling temperature or measuring it independently;
- pass: Delta_nu_i model separates pressure/strain from temperature within uncertainty;
- output: PRESSURE_STRAIN_CALIBRATION_RECORD.

TEST_HPCS_005_HEAT_EXTRACTION_BASELINE_COMPARISON:
- input: unmodified host, active HPCS host, equal heat source, equal sink boundary;
- procedure: run baseline and active configurations under equivalent load;
- pass: q_net_protected_sustained > 0, heat_burden_coverage declared, and baseline equivalence passes;
- output: HEAT_EXTRACTION_RECORD.

TEST_HPCS_006_POLARITON_ROUTE_DETECTION:
- input: polar material stack, IR band, thermal or optical excitation;
- procedure: measure attenuation, resonance condition, and path loss across the intended lane;
- pass: route condition and path-length model are consistent with measured response;
- output: POLARITON_ROUTE_RECORD.

TEST_HPCS_007_FIELD_ASSISTED_PARASITIC_AUDIT:
- input: RF/microwave/near-field/time-modulated source and field-responsive layer;
- procedure: measure internal and external parasitic heat with field on/off;
- pass: active benefit remains positive after q_parasitic_internal and q_parasitic_external are debited;
- output: FIELD_PARASITIC_RECORD.

TEST_HPCS_008_INTERNAL_LASING_THRESHOLD:
- input: gain candidate, pump path, feedback path, output collector;
- procedure: sweep pump and record output power, linewidth, threshold, residual heat, and reversibility;
- pass: P_out_laser >= P_out_laser_min, threshold behavior exists, residual heat is within limit;
- output: LASER_THRESHOLD_RECORD.

TEST_HPCS_009_DAMAGE_INTERRUPT_WITNESS:
- input: heat-path or optical-path coupon with controlled crack/delamination/damage source;
- procedure: compare pre-damage and post-damage optical/thermal path signals;
- pass: damage produces a detectable, bounded, and nonambiguous change;
- output: DAMAGE_WITNESS_RECORD.

TEST_HPCS_010_COMBINED_HARSH_ENVIRONMENT_CYCLE:
- input: candidate stack, combined temperature/pressure/strain/cycling plan;
- procedure: run combined exposure, then repeat baseline optical, thermal, and material-compatibility tests;
- pass: stack remains within calibration and survivability envelope;
- output: COMBINED_HARSH_CYCLE_RECORD.

## 14.7 MINIMUM_VIABLE_EXPERIMENT_PACKAGES
The first construction work should start with the smallest article that can mark as disproven in envelope a false claim.

MVE_001_OPTICAL_WITNESS_BASELINE:
Goal: prove that a candidate marker produces a repeatable optical feature in a benign environment.
Required article: host coupon, active marker, optical access path, baseline environment record.
Required measurements: feature position, intensity, linewidth, background, repeatability, uncertainty.
Promotes only: optical-witness candidate inside baseline envelope.

MVE_002_READOUT_HEATING_AUDIT:
Goal: prove the probe does not dominate the measured response.
Required article: same as MVE_001 with power sweep and dwell-time variation.
Required measurements: feature shift versus readout power, local temperature proxy, drift, recovery.
Promotes only: safe readout lane and maximum allowed readout power.

MVE_003_THERMAL_CYCLE_SURVIVAL:
Goal: prove the marker and host survive heating/cooling cycles.
Required article: host plus marker plus barrier or mount.
Required measurements: pre/post spectral feature, visual or microscopy inspection where available, mass or dimensional change where available.
Promotes only: thermal-cycle candidate envelope.

MVE_004_PRESSURE_OR_STRAIN_CALIBRATION:
Goal: prove pressure or strain can be inferred from a measured feature.

## 14.9 FIRST_BUILD_RECOMMENDATION
The first practical build should be MVE_001 followed by MVE_002 using the simplest available Raman or IR marker coupon.

Recommended first branch:
Recipe A or Recipe C from Section 14.2.

Reason:
The project must prove stable optical readout and readout-heating bounds before heat extraction, pressure calibration, field-assisted transport, or internal lasing can be interpreted correctly.

Do not start with internal lasing, near-field pumping, or heat extraction until the optical witness baseline and readout-heating audit pass.

## 14.10 TERMINATION_RULES
Stop the test and mark the result DISPROVEN_IN_TEST_ENVELOPE or NEEDS_REDESIGN when any of the following occurs:

- marker feature disappears;
- background exceeds feature resolution;
- readout power changes the result more than the environmental variable;
- sample cracks, delaminates, reacts, or darkens;
- sink saturates;
- temperature rises without a declared heat path;
- pressure fixture changes geometry outside tolerance;
- pump or field source adds uncontrolled heat;
- output signal is explained by fluorescence or thermal glow rather than the declared branch;
- uncertainty exceeds the acceptance threshold.


## 14.11 TRAP_STORAGE_BUILD_PATH
[ENGINEERING_STANDARD_RULE] The first trap-storage build path shall not begin with a pure optical cavity as the primary storage claim. It shall begin with a measurable material storage state and may then add virtual-cavity gating or readout.

[REQUIRES_TEST] Recommended trap-storage experiment order:
- TMS_EXP_001: baseline material storage test using latent heat, phase-state storage, or another declared reservoir;
- TMS_EXP_002: capture/leakage measurement with no release trigger;
- TMS_EXP_003: controlled release trigger test;
- TMS_EXP_004: virtual-cavity or polaritonic gate added as selector or release coupler;
- TMS_EXP_005: optical or narrowband release channel qualification;
- TMS_EXP_006: cycle, survivability, and energy-balance test.

[REQUIRES_MATERIAL_QUALIFICATION] Preferred first material directions are candidates only:
- ceramic-encapsulated phase-change material with optical readout layer;
- porous ceramic scaffold with qualified storage fill and protective barrier;
- rare-earth or Raman-active inclusion used as release/readout unless separately qualified as storage;
- SiC, sapphire, alumina, or YSZ barrier stack for containment and optical access.

[ENGINEERING_STANDARD_RULE] Minimum trap-storage build package:
- storage coupon;
- heat input control;
- temperature sensors outside and inside the protected zone where available;
- optical or IR readout path;
- release trigger;
- calorimetric or equivalent energy-balance measurement;
- containment and relief strategy;
- cycle test record;
- proof_vector_id;
- test_envelope_id.

[DISPROVE_IF_UNPROVEN] Trap-storage termination rule:
Stop a branch if uncontrolled leakage exceeds useful release, if stored energy cannot be measured, if release cannot be commanded, if the energy balance cannot close, if the proof vector is not tied to the same material and envelope, or if the storage state damages the host before useful release.
